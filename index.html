What do these grades mean?

New York City's Health Department inspects every permitted restaurant, unannounced, at least once a year. Inspectors assign points for violations — fewer points is better. Roughly: 0–13 points earns an A, 14–27 a B, 28 or more a C.

Grade pending means the restaurant scored poorly on an initial inspection and is awaiting the re-inspection that sets its letter — it is not a failing grade. Some inspection types (administrative, pre-permit) never produce a grade at all.

Not yet inspected means the city's dataset shows no inspection on record — common for newly permitted restaurants.

Source: NYC Open Data, “DOHMH New York City Restaurant Inspection Results,” updated daily by the city. This site queries it live and shows it faithfully.
